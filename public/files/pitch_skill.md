# Skill: Presentation Builder

## When to use this skill
Use this skill whenever asked to create a presentation,
pitch deck, or slide deck for any topic or purpose.

## Step 1 — Ask these questions first
Before building anything, ask these questions in one
friendly conversational message. Keep it light and easy.
Short answers are perfectly fine. Wait for their response
before building anything.

---

Before I start building — a few quick questions
so I can make this actually good:

1. Who is going to see this? 
   (e.g. your boss, a client, your whole team)

2. What do you want them to do or feel after seeing it?

3. Pick a vibe — bold and dramatic / clean and professional
   / warm and inspiring / fun and energetic?

4. Any colour, style, or visual feel you love or hate?

5. Is there anything specific you absolutely want in here
   that I might not know about?

Just a few words for each is totally fine.

---

## Step 2 — Build using this structure
Use their answers to shape the tone, language, emphasis,
colour choices, and what gets highlighted most.

Every presentation must have:

### Opening slide
A strong statement — not just a title.
Something that makes the audience want to keep watching.

### Why this matters
Ground the presentation in one specific fact,
number, or insight. Make the audience care immediately.

### The core content
One idea per slide. No exceptions.
Every key claim backed by a specific number or evidence.

### The unexpected insight
One slide with something the audience did not expect.
The slide that makes people lean forward.

### The recommendation
One clear ask or decision. Direct. No hedging.

### The honest risk
Address the hardest question before the audience asks it.
Shows credibility more than anything else.

### Closing
Something memorable. Not "thank you."
A statement or question that stays with them.

## Step 3 — Design
- Clean layout — each slide clearly separated
- Key numbers and data points visually emphasised
- Tone matches what they described in question 3
- Colours and style reflect question 4 where possible
- Output as complete HTML with all CSS included

## What this skill never does
- Start building before asking the questions
- Put more than one idea on a single slide
- Make claims without evidence behind them
- End with "thank you" as the closing slide
- Give the audience more than one thing to act on
